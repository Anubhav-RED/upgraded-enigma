◆ Common Questions
Do I need to already train BJJ or boxing before starting MMA? ↓
No. We have a dedicated MMA beginner track that starts from zero across all disciplines. You'll learn the striking, grappling and wrestling fundamentals within the MMA program itself. Having a background in one art helps, but it's absolutely not required.
How is MMA training different from just doing BJJ and boxing separately? ↓
MMA training specifically drills the transitions between disciplines — the moments where most fighters fail. Shooting a takedown against a striker, defending a takedown and returning to striking, controlling ground position under punches — these are skills that only come from dedicated MMA-specific drilling. Cross-training helps, but only integrated MMA training builds real fight-readiness.
When will I be ready to compete? ↓
Readiness for competition depends on training consistency and natural progression. Most students who train 4+ sessions per week are competition-ready within 12–18 months. Your coaches will advise when you're at the right level — we never push students to compete before they're ready.
Is MMA training safe? ↓
Yes — when structured correctly. All live drilling and sparring is supervised by coaches. Beginners do not participate in live MMA sparring until they have built the fundamental skills to do so safely. Protective gear is worn during all contact sessions. Injury risk is significantly lower in a well-run MMA program than in unstructured gym environments.
Should I train MMA or individual disciplines? ↓
If your goal is competition MMA or complete self-defence, the MMA program is the right choice. If you have a specific passion for one art — pure BJJ, pure Muay Thai — then the individual discipline programs give deeper focus in that art. Many students do both: attend their specialist discipline class plus the MMA integration sessions. Your coach can help you structure this.
