◈ Intel Briefing

Common
Questions.

Do I need any background before starting MMA? +
No. Our beginner MMA track starts from absolute zero across all four disciplines. You'll learn striking, grappling and wrestling within the program itself. Having experience in one art helps — but it's never required before your first class.
How is MMA different from just cross-training BJJ and boxing? +
MMA training drills the transitions — the moments most fighters fail. Shooting a takedown against a live striker, defending and returning to standing, finishing from dominant position under strikes. These skills only come from dedicated integrated MMA training. Cross-training helps; dedicated MMA training builds fighters.
When will I be ready to compete? +
Most students training 4+ sessions per week are competition-ready within 12–18 months. Your coaches determine readiness — we never push anyone before they're fully prepared. When the time comes, we run a complete fight camp and corner you on the day.
Is MMA training safe? +
Yes — when structured correctly. Beginners don't enter live MMA sparring until they've built the fundamentals to do so safely. All contact sessions are coach-supervised with protective gear. Injury risk in a well-run program is significantly lower than unstructured training.
Should I train MMA or focus on one discipline first? +
If competition MMA or complete self-defence is your goal, start MMA directly — the program covers all four disciplines. If you have a passion for pure BJJ or Muay Thai, those programs go deeper in that art. Many students do both. Your coach will help you structure the right approach.
