◆ The Discipline

What Is
BJJ?

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is a ground-based martial art built on the principle that a smaller, technically skilled person can control and submit a larger, stronger opponent using leverage, angles and positional awareness. Developed from traditional Japanese judo and refined in Brazil by the Gracie family, BJJ has become the most intellectually rigorous grappling art in the world.

In modern combat sports, BJJ is the foundation that every serious MMA fighter and grappling competitor builds on. It teaches you to survive from bad positions, work toward dominant positions, and finish fights through chokes and joint locks without relying on brute force. It is called the "gentle art" — not because it is soft, but because its power comes from the mind, not the body.

At Red Crown MMA in Noida, we teach both Gi (traditional kimono) and No-Gi BJJ, giving students fluency across both rule sets and preparing them for every competitive format from IBJJF points tournaments to submission-only and MMA cage competition.

◆ Curriculum

What You'll Learn

01
Positions & Hierarchy
Mount, back, side control, guard — understanding the dominance structure from session one. You will learn why position before submission is not just a phrase, but the core logic of BJJ.
02
Guard Work
Closed, half, butterfly, spider, De La Riva and more. The guard is where BJJ begins — you will learn to attack actively from the bottom and make your guard a genuine threat.
03
Passing the Guard
Pressure passing, torreando, leg-drag, stack and speed passes. Guard passing is where most beginners lose — we address it directly and systematically.
04
Submissions
Rear naked choke, triangle, guillotine, armbar, kimura, leg locks and more. Submissions are the finish — you will drill them from the correct entry positions, not in isolation.
05
Sweeps & Reversals
How to return to top position from inferior positions. Sweeps are the tactical response to a competent passer — you will build a reliable sweep system in your guard.
06
Belt Progression & Grading
Structured grading every 3–6 months. White through black belt, with stripe promotions tracking incremental progress. Every promotion at Red Crown is earned on the mat.
07
Competition Preparation
Points systems, advantages, submission-only rule sets, tournament tactics, cutting weight and mental preparation for first-time and experienced competitors.
08
No-Gi Grappling
Wrestling-influenced entries, underhooks, body lock takedowns and leg lock systems. No-Gi is essential preparation for MMA and submission-only competition.

◆ What to Expect

Inside a Typical Class

01
Warm-Up & Movement

20 minutes of sport-specific movement — shrimping, bridging, breakfalls and positional transitions to prepare joints and establish body awareness.

02
Technique Instruction

The coach demonstrates 2–3 linked techniques with a training partner. You watch, ask questions, then drill the sequence with your partner in controlled reps.

03
Positional Sparring

Live rounds from specific positions — guard, mount, back. Positional rounds let you refine exactly what was drilled rather than going straight to open rolling.

04
Open Rolling

Live sparring from standing or guard. Rounds of 5–6 minutes with rotating partners. This is where the drilling is tested and real understanding develops.


Most classes at Red Crown run 75–90 minutes. Beginners train alongside all levels under supervised instruction — you will not be thrown into advanced rolling without preparation. Your first few weeks focus on fundamental positions, basic submissions and the etiquette of training safely.

Expect to be uncomfortable, then to get better at managing discomfort. Expect to tap frequently and to learn something from every round. The progress curve in BJJ is steep early — most students see dramatic improvement in their first three months of consistent training.
