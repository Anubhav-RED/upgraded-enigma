◆ The Discipline

What Is
Kids Program?

The juniors program at Red Crown combines foundational BJJ (Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu) and Muay Thai striking with the structured character-development framework that martial arts training is uniquely suited to provide. Children learn to move, fall safely, defend themselves intelligently, listen to instruction and treat training partners with respect — all within the same session.

BJJ in the juniors program focuses on positional awareness, safe breakfalls, basic guard and mount positions, and simple submission defence — the tools that make a child genuinely harder to bully without developing aggression. The striking component introduces the Muay Thai stance, jab-cross and teep as tools for distance management and coordination, not for hitting other children in the face.

As children progress through the Red Crown belt system, they build a record of real achievements tied to actual skill development — not just attendance. Every grading at Red Crown requires genuine technical demonstration. Belts here mean something.

◆ Curriculum

What You'll Learn

01
Safe Falling & Movement
Breakfalls, rolls, shrimping and basic wrestling movements — the body literacy that prevents injury in sport and everyday life. Every junior starts here.
02
BJJ Positions
Mount, side control, guard and back position — taught as safe games of positional awareness. Children learn to recognise dominant and inferior positions without aggression.
03
Basic Submissions & Defence
Rear naked choke, armbar and guillotine are introduced in a controlled way alongside the escape and tap-out protocols that make them completely safe to practise.
04
Muay Thai Stance & Striking Fundamentals
The fighting stance, guard position, jab, cross and teep. Striking is taught on pads only — children never strike each other without full protective gear and direct supervision.
05
Anti-Bullying & Real-World Defence
De-escalation skills, awareness and the specific physical tools that help a child disengage from unwanted physical contact. Taught as safety education, not aggression.
06
Belt & Stripe Progression
Junior belts and coloured stripes mark incremental achievement. Grading sessions reward attendance, technique, attitude and respect — reinforcing the values the program teaches.
07
Focus, Discipline & Listening
Following instructions under pressure, staying calm when training is hard, respecting training partners and coaches. The character development happens in the training itself.
08
Team Drills & Partner Work
Games, relay drills and partner exercises that build coordination and teach children to work with others — a social and physical learning environment in every session.

◆ Why Train

Benefits of
Kids Program Training

◆
Confidence That Lasts

Children who train martial arts consistently develop genuine confidence — not false bravado, but the grounded self-assurance that comes from knowing they can handle physical pressure.

◆
Discipline & Focus

Following instructions, waiting their turn, finishing the drill correctly before moving on — martial arts training builds the focus habits that directly benefit academic performance.

◆
Physical Literacy

Coordination, balance, spatial awareness and the ability to move their body deliberately — children who train BJJ and Muay Thai develop physical literacy well beyond their peers.

◆
Anti-Bullying Tools

Children learn to de-escalate, to move away from contact and — when necessary — to protect themselves without escalating. The result is a calmer, more aware child in difficult social situations.

Bowing in, using titles, caring about their training partner's safety — the ritual of martial arts teaches a form of respect that many modern environments no longer reinforce.

◆
Long-Term Athletic Foundation

The movement patterns, body awareness and grappling coordination developed in children's BJJ and Muay Thai are transferable to virtually every sport they pursue later.

◆ What to Expect

Inside a Typical Class

01
Warm-Up Games & Movement

Age-appropriate movement games that build coordination, get the body ready and establish the energy and focus needed for the session ahead.

02
Technique & Drilling

Coach-led instruction with demonstration, then partner drilling of 2–3 related techniques. Corrections are positive, specific and delivered at the child's level.

03
Positional Games or Pad Work

Grappling positional games or striking pad work — depending on the session focus. This is the live-application phase where the drilled technique is tested in a controlled way.

04
Closing Routine & Character Talk

Cool-down, line-up, bow-out, and a brief reflection on the session's character theme — whether respect, persistence, focus or teamwork.


Junior classes run 60 minutes. The structure is deliberately varied to maintain attention — no single activity lasts longer than 12–15 minutes. Coaches use positive reinforcement throughout and keep the energy constructive and focused.

Parents are welcome to watch from the side. Children who have never trained before are partnered with experienced juniors who know how to be helpful training partners. Most children are actively engaged within 15 minutes of their first class.
