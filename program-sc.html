◆ The Discipline

What Is
Muay Thai?

Muay Thai — also known as the Art of Eight Limbs — is Thailand's national combat sport and the world's most complete stand-up striking system. Unlike Western boxing, which uses only the fists, or kickboxing, which adds leg kicks, Muay Thai deploys all eight weapons of the human body: both fists, both feet, both knees, and both elbows.

The clinch game in Muay Thai is what separates it from every other striking art. From the Thai plum (neck tie), fighters control the opponent's posture, land short knees and elbows, and set up throws and sweeps. This makes it uniquely dangerous at close range — and uniquely effective in MMA, where clinch control determines the flow of a fight.

At Red Crown MMA in Noida, we teach Muay Thai with full fidelity to the traditional system while adapting training methods to modern combat sports science. Pad work, bag work, clinch drilling and controlled sparring are all part of a complete class structure used by every student regardless of level.

◆ Curriculum

What You'll Learn

01
Stance, Footwork & Guard
Orthodox and southpaw stance, guard position, weight distribution and the fundamental footwork patterns — advancing, retreating, pivoting and circling. The foundation everything else is built on.
02
The Jab, Cross, Hook, Uppercut
The four punches of Muay Thai, each with full biomechanical instruction — hip rotation, shoulder drive, elbow position and guard hand placement on every shot.
03
Elbows
Horizontal, diagonal, downward, spinning and upward elbows. Range and entry points for each, with pad drilling to develop speed, accuracy and impact generation.
04
Teep (Push Kick) & Round Kick
The teep as a range tool and fight management weapon. The round kick as the primary power technique — hip turn, standing leg, foot position and the full hip snap.
05
Knee Strikes
Single and double knees from the clinch, long-range knees off the step-in, and flying knees for advanced students. Knees from the Thai plum control position.
06
Clinch Control
Neck tie, double collar tie, over-under, body lock. Controlling posture, landing inside knees and elbows, off-balancing and sweeping from clinch.
07
Pad Work & Combinations
Coach-led pad rounds with pre-set combinations and open pad work. Building timing, rhythm, output and the habit of working behind the jab.
08
Defence & Countering
Blocking, parrying, rolling, checking leg kicks, evading and the defensive footwork that keeps you safe long enough to counter.

◆ What to Expect

Inside a Typical Class

01
Warm-Up & Shadow Boxing

Dynamic stretching, footwork drills and 3 rounds of shadow boxing to rehearse movement patterns and dial in combinations before making contact.

02
Technical Instruction

Coach demonstrates 2–3 linked techniques. Students drill them with a partner in repetitive rounds, with coach corrections on mechanics throughout.

03
Pad Work or Bag Rounds

Heavy bag work for self-directed drilling, or coach pad rounds for more advanced students. This is where power, timing and output are developed.

04
Clinch & Sparring

Clinch drilling followed by controlled sparring rounds — light for beginners, progressive for experienced students. Always coach-supervised.


Classes run 75–90 minutes and are structured to build each skill on the last. Beginners start in dedicated beginner sessions where the pace is deliberately controlled — you will not be thrown into full-contact sparring on week one.

Expect soreness in muscles you did not know existed. Expect to be corrected on your guard, your hip turn and your footwork repeatedly. That correction is the product — the attention to mechanics is what separates Muay Thai training at Red Crown from shadow-boxing fitness classes.
