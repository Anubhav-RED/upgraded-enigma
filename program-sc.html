◆ The Discipline

What Is
S&C?

Strength and conditioning for combat sports is a specialised field that most gym coaches and personal trainers are not equipped to deliver correctly. The demands of MMA, BJJ and Muay Thai competition require athletes to express maximal strength and speed in very short bursts, sustain aerobic output over 15–25 minute bouts, and recover rapidly between rounds — all while maintaining technique under fatigue.

Standard gym programming optimises for one quality: size, or one-rep maximum strength, or cardio endurance. Combat S&C optimises for all of them simultaneously and in the right proportions. This requires periodisation — planned cycles of training emphasis that prepare an athlete to peak at the right time — and a coach who understands how S&C interacts with technical martial arts training load.

At Red Crown MMA Academy in Noida, our S&C program is delivered by coaches who are also martial artists. They understand the training calendar, the physical demands of each discipline and how to build the athlete without destroying the technician. The result is fighters who arrive at competition physically prepared to execute everything they have drilled.

◆ Curriculum

What You'll Learn

01
Maximal Strength Phase
Compound movements — deadlift, squat, press and pull — trained in low rep ranges to build the structural strength that generates power and prevents injury. Combat-athlete-specific loading patterns.
02
Explosive Power Development
Olympic lifting derivatives (hang cleans, push press), med ball work, plyometrics and loaded jumps that develop the rate-of-force development needed for takedowns, kicks and explosive cage exchanges.
03
Aerobic Base Building
Cardiac output work that develops the aerobic engine required to sustain high-intensity output across multiple rounds without accumulating the fatigue that causes technical breakdown.
04
Lactic Threshold & Alactic Power
HIIT protocols calibrated to the work-rest ratios of combat sports — developing the capacity to repeatedly express power in short bursts and recover in the time between rounds.
05
Injury Prevention & Prehab
Shoulder prehab, neck conditioning, hip flexor and glute development, knee stability work — the structural resilience training that keeps fighters healthy through training camp and competition.
06
Mobility & Flexibility
Sport-specific mobility for grappling and striking — hip range for kicks, shoulder mobility for guillotine defence, thoracic extension for sprawling. Functional flexibility, not static stretching.
07
Recovery Protocols
Deload week planning, HRV monitoring basics, sleep optimisation and the management of training load across technical and S&C sessions to prevent overtraining.
08
Fight Camp Periodisation
How to structure a 6–12 week fight camp — building the base, sharpening intensity, peaking on fight week and making weight without catastrophic energy loss in the final days.

◆ What to Expect

Inside a Typical Class

01
Activation & Priming

Joint mobility, targeted activation drills and CNS priming specific to the day's session focus. Not a generic warm-up — a preparation phase matched to the training stimulus.

02
Primary Strength or Power Work

The main training stimulus of the session — maximal strength, explosive power, or specific energy system work. Performed at full quality while the athlete is fresh.

03
Auxiliary & Supplementary Work

Accessory exercises targeting injury prevention, weak points and supporting structures. This phase builds the physical robustness that the primary work depends on.

04
Conditioning Finisher

Session-specific conditioning work calibrated to the week's load — aerobic, lactic or alactic depending on the training phase. Not random cardio — purpose-built output work.
