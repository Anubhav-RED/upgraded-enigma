The Discipline

What Is
Mixed Martial Arts?

Mixed Martial Arts is the most complete combat sport in competitive history. Unlike single-discipline martial arts that operate at one range or in one context, MMA deliberately integrates stand-up striking, clinch fighting, wrestling and ground grappling into a single, fluid system. A well-trained MMA fighter is dangerous standing, in the clinch, on the way to the ground, and once the fight reaches the mat.

MMA competition — as seen in the UFC, ONE Championship and Bellator — demands that athletes are functional in every position, because opponents are specifically trained to exploit any gap in your skill set. The striker who cannot grapple gets taken down and controlled. The wrestler who cannot strike gets picked apart on the feet. The BJJ player who cannot clinch gets held at range and outpointed. The complete MMA fighter addresses every range — and our MMA training in Noida is structured to build exactly that.

Beyond competition, MMA training produces the most well-rounded physical and technical development of any martial art. Students build striking precision, grappling athleticism, cardiovascular endurance and the composure to perform under pressure — qualities that carry far beyond the mat.

Curriculum

What You'll Learn
in MMA Classes.

01
MMA-Specific Striking
The MMA jab as a range tool, not just a damage shot. Punching into a takedown entry. Striking off the cage. Defending shots while throwing. Stand-up in MMA is a completely different discipline from pure boxing or Muay Thai — it is taught as such at Red Crown, with the takedown threat always present in every exchange.
02
Takedowns & Takedown Defence
Shooting for a takedown against a live striker — double leg, single leg and body lock — timed off combinations where the opponent's striking rhythm creates the entry. Equally, sprawl mechanics, hip defence and hand fighting for fighters who prefer to keep the fight standing.
03
Cage & Clinch Wrestling
Offensive and defensive cage wrestling — underhooks, body lock, inside trips, creating separation and landing on the exit. Cage work is an MMA-specific skill and cannot be fully replicated in Muay Thai or wrestling training alone. It is drilled in dedicated blocks at Red Crown.
04
Ground & Pound
Top position strikes from mount, side control and back control — landing damage while maintaining dominant grappling position and not losing control. The intersection of striking and grappling that defines MMA's ground game and separates pure BJJ from the complete MMA skill set.
05
Submission Hunting Under Strikes
Finding and finishing submissions from dominant positions while managing the strike threat. Triangle from guard, rear naked choke from back control, armbars and kimuras from top position — all drilled in the context of a live MMA round where the opponent can also punch.
06
Getting Back to Your Feet
Returning to standing from bottom position under active striking threat — guard, guard retention, standing from turtle position and creating exit angles against a top-pressure opponent. This is the most neglected skill in MMA training and one of the most important in a real fight.
07
Integrated MMA Sparring
Live MMA rounds where every discipline is active simultaneously. Positional rounds — starting in the clinch, from guard, from back position — build specific competency. Open MMA sparring develops fight IQ, timing and the ability to perform under live, unpredictable pressure. All sparring is progressive and coach-supervised.
08
Fight Camp Preparation
For students pursuing competition: structured eight to twelve week camps specific to an opponent's style, physical preparation, weight management strategy, mental preparation protocols and corner coaching. Red Crown sources quality sparring partners across the Delhi-NCR region and backs its fighters on fight day.

What to Expect

Inside a Typical
MMA Class.

01
Warm-Up & Movement

Sport-specific movement — sprawl reactions, level changes, clinch pummelling, guard recovery. Preparing the body for the specific demands of integrated MMA training, not a generic group warm-up.

02
Technique & Drilling

Coach-led instruction on two to three linked techniques — a striking sequence that transitions into a takedown entry, a takedown into a submission attempt. Partner drilling with progressive resistance builds the movement pattern before live application.

03
Positional Rounds

Live rounds from specific MMA positions — cage clinch, guard with strikes, standing with takedown threat live. Positional rounds build targeted competency in the scenarios that matter most before open sparring begins.

04
Open MMA Sparring

Full MMA rounds — all positions and ranges live simultaneously. For beginners, open sparring is replaced with focused technical rounds until the mechanics are sound enough to benefit from it. Always coach-supervised, always progressive.
