◆ The Discipline

What Is
Wrestling?

Wrestling is the oldest combat sport in recorded history, practised across virtually every culture on earth. In its modern competitive forms — freestyle and Greco-Roman — it focuses on takedowns, trips, throws and mat control, with the objective of pinning the opponent or accumulating points through successful attacks.

In the context of MMA, wrestling is categorically the most important grappling skill. Statistical analysis of professional MMA consistently shows that the wrestler — the fighter who can choose to take the fight to the ground or keep it standing — holds the decisive tactical advantage. The sprawl-and-brawl style, the cage-pressure takedown, the lat-step entry off a jab — all of these are wrestling expressions applied in the cage.

At Red Crown MMA in Noida, we teach freestyle and Greco-Roman wrestling entries alongside the cage-specific wrestling techniques that apply directly in MMA competition. Students who are not interested in fighting also train wrestling for its physical benefits — wrestling conditioning is among the most demanding and rewarding in all of sport.

◆ Curriculum

What You'll Learn

01
Double Leg Takedown
The most common takedown in MMA. Setup from a jab or level change, the penetration step, driving through the hips, clearing the hips to the mat and transitioning to top control.
02
Single Leg Takedown
Outside and inside single leg entries, run the pipe finish, trip finish and the high-crotch transition. Wrestling on one leg under resistance — a fundamental scramble skill.
03
Greco-Roman Clinch
Body lock, over-under, two-on-one and collar tie. Greco is the foundation for the clinch wrestling that dominates MMA — particularly against the cage.
04
Sprawl Defence
Reading the entry, setting the sprawl, cross-body pressure and returning to standing. Takedown defence is a wrestling skill, not just a reaction skill.
05
Cage Wrestling
Offensive and defensive use of the cage wall — pinning, positioning, digging underhooks, landing short knees in the clinch and creating exit angles.
06
Scrambles & Leg Entanglements
The chaotic positions that follow a failed takedown or guard escape. Reading scramble positions, protecting your back, securing top position in transition.
07
Guard Passing for Wrestlers
Pressure-based passing from wrestling-familiar positions — stack, knee slice and leg drag entries that wrestlers can access naturally from their top control instincts.
08
MMA-Specific Takedowns
Shooting in against a live striker. Level-change timing behind the jab, entries under punch counters, pulling guard strategically and the decision model for when to shoot.

Wrestling Training

◆
Tactical Control

Wrestling gives you the decision — stand up or go to the ground. The fighter who controls environment controls the pace, the damage and the outcome.

Shooting for takedowns demands explosive hip power, fast-twitch muscle activation and reactive strength. Wrestling training builds athletic power that transfers to every other sport.

Freestyle wrestling, Greco-Roman, submission wrestling and MMA — wrestling skills open multiple competition pathways. Red Crown prepares students for all of them.
